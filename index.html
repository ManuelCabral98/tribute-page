Alan Mathison Turing

Father of computer science

Here's a timeline of Alan Turing's life and most important achievments

  • 1931 earn a £80 per annum scholarship to study at King's college, Cambridge.
  • 1937 publishes his first paper, a one-page article called Equivalence of left and right almost periodicity (sent on 23 April).
  • 1938 worked part-time with the Government Code and Cypher School (GC&CS), the British codebreaking organisation.
